OTA Listing Examples — Style Reference
These are illustrative examples showing the correct structure, tone, and length
for listings on each platform. Use these as templates when writing new descriptions.

VIATOR EXAMPLES
Viator Example 1 — Evening Food Tour (Food & Drink)
Body:
Make the most of your evening in San Sebastián with access to the city's best pintxos bars—the ones locals actually go to. Skip the tourist strip and let a local guide take you through the old town's narrow streets to five hand-picked stops, each chosen for quality over fame. Finish the night at a standing bar where the txakoli has been poured the same way for decades.
Bullets:

Taste your way through five authentic pintxos bars away from the tourist crowds
A glass of local txakoli wine is included at each stop for the full Basque experience
Small group of 8 maximum means personal attention and real conversation
Flexible departure times available — choose the slot that suits your evening

What makes this Viator-style:

Body is 72 words — short and punchy
Opens with "Make the most of" — action forward
"the ones locals actually go to" — insider FOMO trigger in sentence one
4 bullets: exclusive access → included extra → group size → logistics convenience


Viator Example 2 — Historic Walking Tour (Culture & History)
Body:
Get behind the velvet rope at Florence's most visited sites with a guide whose knowledge goes well beyond the standard script. Skip the queues entirely with pre-booked timed entry and spend your time looking at the art, not the back of someone's head. This is the Uffizi the way it was meant to be experienced — unhurried, intimate, and genuinely illuminating.
Bullets:

Skip-the-line timed entry included — no queuing, straight to the galleries
Expert art historian guide reveals the stories most visitors never hear
Groups capped at 10 people for a museum experience that actually feels peaceful
Upgrade to a private tour for a fully personalised itinerary

What makes this Viator-style:

Body is 68 words
"behind the velvet rope" — exclusivity hook immediately
Bullet 4 is an upsell — Viator listings often include an upgrade option
Tone is confident, slightly premium


VIATOR STRUCTURAL TEMPLATE
[Action opener] + [destination] + [biggest exclusive/access hook] — [FOMO or insider note].
[What the guide unlocks that you couldn't get alone].
[Vivid sensory or "you are there" closing moment].

BULLETS (4 only):
- [Rarest or most exclusive element of the experience]
- [Valuable inclusion beyond the headline]
- [Logistics or convenience benefit — skip the line, small group, flexible time]
- [Upgrade option or added personalisation]
Word count: 60–90 words for body. No more.
Bullets: Exactly 4. Each ~15 words. Start with a verb or strong noun.

GET YOUR GUIDE EXAMPLES
GYG Example 1 — Historic City Walk (Authoritative / Cultural)
Body:
Step into the layered history of Vienna on this walking tour through the Imperial City's most storied streets and squares. Pass the Hofburg Palace, the Spanish Riding School, and the grand facades of the Ringstrasse, as your guide brings seven centuries of Habsburg power to life. Hear the stories behind the architecture that shaped an empire — the court intrigues, the political marriages, the music that defined a continent. From the ornate interior of St. Stephen's Cathedral to the quiet corners of the Burggarten where Mozart once walked, every stop reveals a city that rewards those who look closely. The tour concludes at the Vienna State Opera, steps from the U-Bahn and a short walk from the city's finest coffee houses, should you wish to continue your afternoon in true Viennese style.
What makes this GYG-style:

128 words — much longer than Viator, fully narrative
No bullets — continuous flowing prose
Every landmark named in full
Ends with wayfinding and a lifestyle suggestion
Formal, authoritative tone — no contractions


GYG Example 2 — Food & Social Tour (Casual / Experience)
Body:
What better way to discover Rome than to eat your way through it? On this Roman Street Food Tour, your guide will meet you at the foot of the Pantheon and lead you through the backstreets of the Centro Storico to five of the neighbourhood's most beloved food stops. You'll try supplì at a family-run fryer that's been feeding the neighbourhood since 1961, taste pecorino at a deli where the owner still cuts every slice by hand, and wash it all down with a glass of local white at a standing wine bar that most tourists walk straight past. This is a public tour, and the group — never more than 12 people — has a way of becoming something more than strangers by the end of the evening. So come hungry, come curious, and come ready to eat like a Roman.
What makes this GYG-style:

148 words — long form, fully narrative
Opens with a rhetorical question
Specific vivid detail: "since 1961", "still cuts every slice by hand"
Group size framed as a social feature
Warm, enthusiastic close
No bullets anywhere


GET YOUR GUIDE STRUCTURAL TEMPLATES
For historic / cultural tours:
[Evocative verb] into [destination] on this [tour type] through [area description].
[Name every landmark in the first 2 sentences].
[What the guide reveals — stories, secrets, context].
[Specific hidden detail or lesser-known moment].
[Continue through stops — full proper nouns throughout].
[End with wayfinding + lifestyle close near a landmark or transport hub].
For food / social / experience tours:
[Rhetorical question opener]: "What better way to [discover/experience] [destination] than to [activity]?"
[Meeting point — specific location, neighbourhood named].
[What you'll do — number of stops, types of venue, specific products].
[A specific vivid detail that proves authenticity — year founded, family-run, hand-cut].
[Social proof / group dynamic — frame size as a feature, not a limit].
[Warm energetic close — idiom, invitation, call to feel something].
Word count: 120–200 words for body.
Tone: Either fully authoritative (history/culture) or fully casual (food/social). Never mix.
Bullets: None in the main body — GYG uses a separate field for structured highlights.
